Radiation defines the limits of human survival.

Ionizing radiation damages DNA, disrupts cellular function, and accumulates invisibly over time. Once exposure occurs, its biological consequences cannot be undone.

In space, radiation limits how far and how long humans can travel. In medicine, it saves lives while permanently damaging healthy tissue. Yet protection remains largely passive.

Space Exploration

Beyond low Earth orbit, radiation is the primary health risk to astronauts. NASA identifies radiation exposure as the leading barrier to long-duration missions.

Radiation-Based Medicine

Over 17 million radiation therapy treatments are delivered globally each year. While radiation destroys cancer cells, it also damages surrounding healthy tissue.

Over 20 million people each year face the same biological challenge.
